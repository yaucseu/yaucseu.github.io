Access Guide

General ideas

See Wikitravel. Popular navigation apps besides Google Maps are Maps.app in OSX & iOS (in English) and Autonavi (in Chinese).

To the Sìpáilóu campus, from the airport

The airplanes land on Nánjīng Lùkǒu International Airport, 南京禄口国际机场.

  • By taxi

    •     The official taxi stand is outside the airport door. Those who talk to you in the airport are not official, and will not give you the proper receipt.

        Ask the driver to bring you to the Sìpáilóu campus of Southeast University. The campus has three gates:

    • The biggest is the South gate, 南门。
    • Our office is near the East gate, 东门。
    • Liúyuán hotel is near the West gate, 榴园门。

    The taxi fare is 140-180 yuan (20-25 USD), depending on the traffic and time of the day. The driver may ask you 10 yuan for a highway ticket along the way. Before getting off, ask the driver to give a receipt (in addition to the highway ticket) if necessary.

    请送我到东南大学四牌楼校区南门。请记得给我发票。

  • By metro

    •     The metro working hours are roughly 06:00 - 22:00 (later in the city center).

    1. Take Metro No.S1 until the last stop, South Railway station, 南京南站.
    2. Change to Metro No.3 to Línchǎng, 林场, and get off at Jīmíngsì, 鸡鸣寺.
    3. Leave the metro station at the exit No.1 or No.6 of Jīmíngsì.
    4. Walk towards the East Gate of Sìpáilóu campus. The campus gates are open for 24 hours.

To the Sìpáilóu campus, from railway stations

The high-speed railway stops either at Nánjīng (North) Railway station, 南京站 or Nánjīng South Railway station, 南京南站.

  • From Nánjīng (North) Railway station

    • Take Metro No.3 to Mòzhōu Dōnglù, 秣周东路, and get off at Jīmíngsì, 鸡鸣寺.

  • From Nánjīng South Railway station

    • Take Metro No.3 to Línchǎng, 林场, and get off at Jīmíngsì, 鸡鸣寺.

Inside the campus

  1. Shing-Tung Yau Center of Southeast University, 东南大学丘成桐中心, is on the 15th floor of Shaw Architectural Center (Yìfū Jiànzhú Guǎn), 逸夫建筑馆15楼.
  2. If the door of the building is closed, wait until someone opens it.
  3. Liúyuán Hotel, 榴园宾馆, near the West Gate is about 500m from Yau Center.

We booked Liúyuán Hotel for the non-Chinese invited speakers, and those who requested booking. More information about Liúyuán Hotel can be found in Ctrip (Chinese) and Kayak

Travel tips

  • Cash

    • Credit cards are rarely accepted, except for Starbucks. It is important to get cash at the airport either by withdrawal or by an exchange. Check your condition for overseas withdrawal in advance.

  • Credit card via AliPay

    • There seems to be a workaround for using credit card in China. First you download and install the international version of AliPay app on your smart phone. Then you register your passport and a foreign credit card. Finally you top up by using Tour pass.

    (Warning: I have never tried this method).

  • WiFi

    • To use the free WiFi at the Nanjing (and Shanghai) airport, you need to find a ticket machine for registration (or go to Starbucks). Public WiFi is not ubiquitous in the city.

  • Mobile network

    • The SIM card for travelers is not sold in Nanjing. If you want to use your mobile, purchase a SIM card for travelers before coming to China. Such cards can cross the Great Firewall, but will become invalid after several days.

  • Language

    • Not many people in Nanjing speak English. However, they carry mobile phones, and may have installed translation apps. Note that Google Translate works in China.

  • Air

    • Beware the yellow dust from Central Asia in winter. Wear a mask if you are allergic.

  • Tourism in Nanjing

    • Information in English can be found at e.g. Wikitravel, Nanjing Municipal Tourism Commission, Nanjing city

  • Toilet

    Small restaurants do not have toilets. There are many public toilets on the street, though possibly without toilet papers. Buy and bring your own.
